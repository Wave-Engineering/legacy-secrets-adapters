The Cone of Silence

a low-effort, RAM-only zone your secret never leaves

legacy-secrets-adapters · a walkthrough

The problem

A legacy app reads its credentials from a plaintext file on disk — and won't be re-released. Anyone who reads the disk (a backup, an image layer, a stray git add, a stolen drive) reads the password. We fix it WITHOUT touching the app.

Engage the Cone

Decrypt the secret into a RAM-backed file at the path the app already reads.

[you@host cone-of-silence]$ ./cone.py engage
→ Cone ENGAGED: secret decrypted into RAM at /dev/shm/cone-of-silence/secrets.json (mode 0400)

Cone ENGAGED · Read the secret

The Cone of Silence is in place. Our legacy app reaches her secret the old
cleartext way — with no knowledge or awareness of encryption.
Only the ENCRYPTED file lives on disk; let's look:

[you@host cone-of-silence]$ ls -la
total 88
drwxr-xr-x 3 you you  4096 Jan  1 09:00 .
-rwxr-xr-x 1 you you  5102 Jan  1 09:00 cone.py
-rwxr-xr-x 1 you you  9120 Jan  1 09:00 demonstrate.py
-rw-r--r-- 1 you you 12880 Jan  1 09:00 enlighten.html
-rwxr-xr-x 1 you you  1903 Jan  1 09:00 legacy_reader.py
-rw-r--r-- 1 you you   228 Jan  1 09:00 secrets.json.enc
-rw-r--r-- 1 you you  3650 Jan  1 09:00 walkthrough.py

...yet a cleartext secret is readable. Where? A memory-only filesystem —
the Cone of Silence. Here are the tmpfs (RAM) mounts:

[you@host cone-of-silence]$ df -h -t tmpfs
Filesystem      Size  Used Avail Use% Mounted on
tmpfs           2.8G  4.5M  2.8G   1% /run
tmpfs            14G  4.0K   14G   1% /dev/shm
tmpfs           5.0M     0  5.0M   0% /run/lock
tmpfs           2.8G  5.1M  2.8G   1% /run/user/1000

Our legacy_reader.py starts up exactly as she always has:

[you@host cone-of-silence]$ ./legacy_reader.py --config /dev/shm/cone-of-silence/secrets.json
[legacy-reader] opened /dev/shm/cone-of-silence/secrets.json
[legacy-reader] connecting to db.internal/appdb as app_pg_user ...
[legacy-reader] (password used in memory to connect — never printed, never logged)

And via a softlink at her HARD-CODED path, she runs with NO arguments, too! —
So apps with hard-coded filepaths are supported as well:

[you@host cone-of-silence]$ ./legacy_reader.py
[legacy-reader] opened legacy-etc/secrets.json
[legacy-reader] connecting to db.internal/appdb as app_pg_user ...
[legacy-reader] (password used in memory to connect — never printed, never logged)

legacy_reader_py reads her secret. Nothing on disk changed.

Cone ENGAGED · Detect cleartext

The app just read the secret. So it must be on disk somewhere, right?
Lets hunt through the whole directory for the password:

[you@host cone-of-silence]$ grep -rn 'S3cr3t-Pg-Pass' . --exclude='*.py' --exclude='*.pyc' --exclude='*.html' --exclude='*.md' --exclude-dir='__pycache__' || echo ' (nothing found on disk)'
    (nothing found on disk)

Nothing on disk. The cleartext lives ONLY inside the Cone (RAM). Proof:

[you@host cone-of-silence]$ cat /dev/shm/cone-of-silence/secrets.json
{
  "username": "app_pg_user",
  "passwd": "S3cr3t-Pg-Pass",
  "host": "db.internal",
  "dbname": "appdb"
}
[you@host cone-of-silence]$ df -h /dev/shm/cone-of-silence/secrets.json
Filesystem      Size  Used Avail Use% Mounted on
tmpfs            14G  4.0K   14G   1% /dev/shm

Readable by the app, invisible to the disk. That is the whole trick.

Disengage the Cone

Wipe the secret from RAM. On disk there was only ciphertext all along.

[you@host cone-of-silence]$ ./cone.py disengage
→ Cone DISENGAGED: plaintext wiped from RAM (a reboot wipes /dev/shm anyway)

Cone DISENGAGED · Read the secret

The Cone of Silence is lifted. The secret has evaporated from RAM.
Only ciphertext remains on disk:

[you@host cone-of-silence]$ ls -la
total 88
drwxr-xr-x 3 you you  4096 Jan  1 09:00 .
-rwxr-xr-x 1 you you  5102 Jan  1 09:00 cone.py
-rwxr-xr-x 1 you you  9120 Jan  1 09:00 demonstrate.py
-rw-r--r-- 1 you you 12880 Jan  1 09:00 enlighten.html
-rwxr-xr-x 1 you you  1903 Jan  1 09:00 legacy_reader.py
-rw-r--r-- 1 you you   228 Jan  1 09:00 secrets.json.enc
-rw-r--r-- 1 you you  3650 Jan  1 09:00 walkthrough.py

Watch the legacy app try to start with the Cone disengaged — no secret to be had:

[you@host cone-of-silence]$ ./legacy_reader.py --config /dev/shm/cone-of-silence/secrets.json
!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!
[legacy-reader] FATAL: cannot find my config file: /dev/shm/cone-of-silence/secrets.json
[legacy-reader] No secret, no service. (Is the Cone disengaged?)
!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!

It is the same at her hard-coded path — the softlink is gone, so she fails loudly:

[you@host cone-of-silence]$ ./legacy_reader.py
!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!
[legacy-reader] FATAL: cannot find my config file: legacy-etc/secrets.json
[legacy-reader] No secret, no service. (Is the Cone disengaged?)
!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!

No Cone, no service — loud, early failures make it easy to see what happened.

Cone DISENGAGED · Detect cleartext

The Cone is lifted. Hunt the disk for the password once more:

[you@host cone-of-silence]$ grep -rn 'S3cr3t-Pg-Pass' . --exclude='*.py' --exclude='*.pyc' --exclude='*.html' --exclude='*.md' --exclude-dir='__pycache__' || echo ' (nothing found on disk)'
    (nothing found on disk)

And the Cone itself is empty:

[you@host cone-of-silence]$ ls -la /dev/shm/cone-of-silence 2>/dev/null || echo ' (the Cone is gone)'
    (the Cone is gone)

Nothing, anywhere. On disk: only ciphertext. In RAM: nothing.

The takeaway

The secret is never stored in the clear — only briefly present, in RAM, while the app needs it. On disk: ciphertext. In RAM: gone on power-off. The app never changed.

In production the Cone is a systemd RuntimeDirectory tmpfs, and the key never touches disk — sealed to the machine's TPM, or handed over by OpenBao.
